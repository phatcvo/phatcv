Alternative input-output models

The models so far have taken the form $$\xdot = {\bf f}(\bx, \bu), \by = {\bf g}(\bx, \bu, m),$$ where $m$ is some parameterization of the environment or map. These are, in a sense, the right models for simulation. But they do leave something to be desired for control using noisy sensors in potentially novel environments... the prediction of future outputs depends on having an accurate estimate of the state (in world coordinates) and of the map. cite nanomap.

But in this section, I would also like to consider models of the form $$\by[n+1] = f(\by[n],\by[n-1], ..., \bu[n], \bu[n-1, ...).$$ These are the so-called ARX models (auto-regressive models with exogenous inputs).
